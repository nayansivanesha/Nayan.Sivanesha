About Me

I am Nayan Talekar, a Class XI student with a keen interest in rural development, sustainable agriculture, and community-driven progress. During the COVID-19 lockdown, I spent extended time in my native village, where I gained first-hand exposure to village life and the practical realities of agriculture.

This period significantly shaped my perspective and strengthened my interest in rural development initiatives. Observing the challenges faced by farmers, along with the work carried out through my father's NGO, gave me a deeper understanding of the need for sustainable practices and structured community efforts.

Beyond social work, I actively seek knowledge in areas such as life skills, psychology, leadership, and sustainable development. I believe in combining thoughtful learning with practical action and aim to blend innovative ideas with ground-level solutions.

I am driven by the goal of creating a positive impact that extends beyond individual projects and contributes meaningfully to improving the lives of farmers and strengthening rural communities. I firmly believe that lasting development begins at the grassroots, and I aspire to contribute towards building model villages and sustainable rural systems for the future.

My Work

Atal Bhujal Yojana is a flagship initiative of the Ministry of Jal Shakti, Government of India, launched on the 93rd birth anniversary of Atal Bihari Vajpayee. The programme focuses on demonstrating community-led, sustainable groundwater management in water-stressed regions and aims to create scalable models for effective groundwater resource management across selected states, including Gujarat, Haryana, Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, and Uttar Pradesh.

Under this initiative, Model Action For Rural Change (MARCH), an NGO led by my father, served as an implementing partner in Solapur district, covering approximately 113 villages across four tahsils.

As part of my exposure to this project through MARCH, I closely observed and assisted in field-level activities related to community awareness, village interactions, and understanding groundwater management practices. Being present during village visits, discussions, and awareness efforts allowed me to understand how government schemes are implemented at the grassroots level and how community participation plays a vital role in sustainable resource management.

This experience strengthened my interest in rural development and provided me with valuable insight into the practical challenges faced by rural communities regarding water conservation, agriculture, and long-term sustainability.

My Vision

My vision is to contribute to the development of self-reliant and progressive villages where quality education, accessible healthcare, and essential infrastructure are available within the community. Rural areas should have practical facilities such as cold storage for agricultural produce, efficient resource management, and opportunities that reduce the gap between villages and cities.

Coming from a drought-prone region, I strongly believe that sustainable water management must be the first priority for rural development. Water security forms the foundation for stronger agriculture, better livelihoods, and overall village growth.

I aspire to see farmers who are knowledgeable, confident, and economically empowered through sustainable agricultural practices. In such villages, children should be able to access opportunities locally, without the need to migrate to cities for basic services or employment.

Through collaboration with NGOs and partnership with government initiatives, I aim to work on practical, long-term solutions that strengthen rural systems. My goal is to be recognized for meaningful contributions to the agriculture sector that positively impact farmers and rural communities.
